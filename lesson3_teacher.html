Teacher Guidance – Lesson 2
Problem-Solving: Algorithms (CS) vs Workflows (IT)

Overview

Approx. length: 60 minutes Year group: 9 Options Taster

Focus: Explore how algorithms (CS) and workflows (IT) both solve problems using clear steps, decisions, and digital tools.

Learning Objectives

  • Define an algorithm and a workflow.
  • Spot real-life examples of each (school, home, apps).
  • Explain where CS and IT problem-solving overlap.

Lesson Flow (approx.)

Students complete the Do Now silently in Microsoft OneNote (Class Notebook) under “Lesson 2 – Do Now”. No talking until you say “Reveal answers”.
Approx. Time Stage Teacher Guidance (modelling optional) Student Outcome / Evidence
Start (≈ 5 min) Do Now – Silent recap Display Do Now questions + image. Remind pupils to record answers quietly in OneNote. After a few minutes, click Reveal and check two examples verbally. Written answers in OneNote under “Lesson 2 – Do Now”.
≈ 5 – 10 min Introduce focus Ask the class: “How do algorithms and workflows help people solve problems?” Summarise link between logic and organisation. Can state that both use ordered steps and decisions to reach a goal.
≈ 10 – 20 min Knowledge Recap Show the two Knowledge boxes:
• CS – Flowcharts & Decisions (choose-your-own-adventure analogy).
• IT – Digital Forms & Data Flow (mail-sorter analogy).
Encourage students to spot where decisions appear in each.		 Understands purpose of flowcharts and forms as tools for clear problem-solving.
≈ 20 – 30 min Computer Science Activity – Flowchart Design If helpful, demonstrate one example in PowerPoint with basic shapes (Start, Process, Decision). Remind students to keep symbols clear and arrows tidy. Flowchart designed in PowerPoint showing path through maze with a decision box.
Evidence in OneNote: Students use Snipping Tool (Win + Shift + S) to capture their flowchart and paste it under “Lesson 2 – CS Activity”.
≈ 30 – 40 min Information Technology Activity – Peer-Review Form Guide students to Microsoft Forms. Explain purpose: collect constructive feedback on Lesson 1 slides. If needed, model how to add a question type or title. Form created with 4–6 questions (Yes/No, rating, short text).
Evidence in OneNote: Use Snipping Tool to capture the form and responses page; paste under “Lesson 2 – IT Activity”.
≈ 40 – 50 min Key Vocabulary (reinforce after practice) Review terms (Algorithm, Workflow, Decision, Input/Output). Ask: “Where did you see a decision today?” Verbal recall or short note in OneNote if time allows.
≈ 50 – 58 min Exit Ticket / Reflection Display the three questions (Define algorithm, Define workflow, Choose one example). Students respond in OneNote. Completed Exit Ticket saved in Class Notebook.
Final minutes Plenary Summarise: “Both CS and IT rely on clear steps and smart decisions.” Encourage students to notice these in everyday tasks. Verbal share of key learning points.

Resources and Preparation

  • lesson2.html (projected or on student devices)
  • lesson2.png for Do Now
  • Microsoft PowerPoint (for flowcharts)
  • MazeGenerator.net (optional starter)
  • Microsoft Forms for IT task
  • Class Notebook sections ready for Do Now, CS Activity, IT Activity, and Exit Ticket

Support and Extension

  • Support: Provide a flowchart symbol guide or starter template. Pair students for peer help.
  • Extension: Challenge students to add a loop (“repeat until exit found”) or add branching questions to their Form.
  • SEND: Use modelling only if needed; keep visual examples on screen; encourage students to use Snipping Tool independently for evidence.

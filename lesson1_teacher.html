Teacher Guidance – Lesson 1
The Digital World: Where Do Computer Science and IT Meet?

Overview

Approx. length: 60 minutes  Year group: 9 Options Taster

Focus: Understanding the difference and overlap between Computer Science (creating and programming) and Information Technology (using and managing tools).

Learning Objectives

  • Explain what Computer Science and Information Technology mean.
  • Identify everyday examples of each.
  • Describe where the two subjects overlap.

Lesson Flow (approx.)

Students complete the Do Now quietly in Class Notebook / OneNote under “Lesson 1 – Do Now”. No talking until you say “Reveal answers”.
Approx. Time Stage Teacher Guidance ( modelling optional ) Student Outcome
Start (5 min) Do Now – Silent task Display the Do Now image and statements. Remind: “Answer silently in OneNote.” After a few minutes, click Reveal and discuss one or two examples briefly. Written answers in OneNote under ‘Lesson 1 – Do Now’.
≈ 5 – 10 min Introduce the key question Ask: “Where do Computer Science and IT meet?” Summarise: CS builds it, IT uses it. Both rely on logical thinking and creativity. Can describe one main difference between CS and IT.
≈ 10 – 20 min Knowledge Recap Use the two knowledge boxes: • CS – Algorithms (robot chef / recipe analogy). • IT – Workflows (school play analogy). Ask: “What happens if a step is missing?” Understand algorithm vs workflow with examples.
≈ 20 – 30 min Computer Science Activity – Maze Navigator If helpful, briefly model on MazeGenerator.net: show a short path and debugging. Encourage students to test, fix, and shorten their own sequences. Students write and improve a step sequence (N/E/S/W). Practise debugging.
≈ 30 – 40 min Information Technology Activity – Online Safety Slide Show the IT Activity tab. Remind success criteria: Title + 3 tips + 1 image. If needed, model a slide for clarity. One slide titled ‘Stay Safe Online’ with clear design and 3–4 tips.
≈ 40 – 50 min Key Vocabulary (reinforcement) After both activities, revisit the vocab tab (Algorithm, Program, Hardware…). Keep it brisk – ask pupils to link each term to what they just did. Terms understood through recent use.
≈ 50 – 58 min Exit Ticket / Reflection Display the 3 questions (Identify / Explain / Reflect). Allow short written answers in books or OneNote. Clear written explanation of difference + personal preference.
Final minutes Plenary Reinforce: “CS builds it. IT uses it. Both make technology work.” Invite a few reflections. Verbal share-outs; sense of both pathways’ value.

Resources and Preparation

  • lesson1.html (open on projector or student devices)
  • lesson1.png for Do Now image
  • Access to MazeGenerator.net
  • PowerPoint / Google Slides for IT task
  • Exit Ticket template or digital form

Support and Extension

  • Support: Provide example commands (N/E/S/W) or a slide template with placeholders.
  • Extension: Ask students to shorten their maze algorithm or redesign the slide for a different audience.
  • SEND: Model one example if helpful; allow pairs; keep fonts large and contrast high.

Teacher Guidance – Lesson 1
The Digital World: Where Do Computer Science and IT Meet?

Overview

Approx. length: 60 minutes  Year group: 9 Options Taster

Focus: Understand the difference and overlap between Computer Science (creating and programming) and Information Technology (using and managing tools).

Learning Objectives

  • Explain what Computer Science and Information Technology mean.
  • Identify everyday examples of each.
  • Describe where the two subjects overlap.

Lesson Flow (approx.)

Students complete the Do Now quietly in Microsoft OneNote (Class Notebook) under “Lesson 1 – Do Now”. No discussion until you say “Reveal answers”.
Approx. Time Stage Teacher Guidance (modelling optional) Student Outcome / Evidence
Start (5 min) Do Now – Silent task Display the Do Now image and statements. Remind: “Work silently in OneNote.” After a few minutes, click Reveal and discuss briefly. Typed answers in OneNote under “Lesson 1 – Do Now”.
≈ 5 – 10 min Introduce the key question Ask: “Where do Computer Science and IT meet?”
Summarise: CS builds it, IT uses it. Both involve problem-solving and creativity.		 Students can state one main difference between CS and IT.
≈ 10 – 20 min Knowledge Recap Use the two knowledge boxes:
• CS – Algorithms (robot chef / recipe analogy).
• IT – Workflows (school play analogy).
Ask: “What happens if a step is missing?”		 Understands algorithm vs workflow through examples.
≈ 20 – 30 min Computer Science Activity – Maze Navigator If helpful, demonstrate a short example on MazeGenerator.net. Model debugging briefly then release students to try their own. Students create and improve a sequence (N/E/S/W).
Evidence in OneNote: Use Snipping Tool (Windows + Shift + S) to capture their algorithm and paste it under “Lesson 1 – CS Activity”.
≈ 30 – 40 min Information Technology Activity – Online Safety Slide Show the IT Activity tab. Remind success criteria: Title + 3 tips + 1 image. Model a slide if needed for layout and clarity. Slide created titled “Stay Safe Online” with 3–4 tips and a clear visual.
Evidence in OneNote: Use Snipping Tool to capture their finished slide and paste under “Lesson 1 – IT Activity”.
≈ 40 – 50 min Key Vocabulary (reinforcement) Revisit the vocab tab after activities (Algorithm, Program, Hardware…). Ask students to link each term to what they did today. Terms understood and recorded contextually in discussion notes if desired.
≈ 50 – 58 min Exit Ticket / Reflection Display the three questions (Identify / Explain / Reflect). Students answer in OneNote under “Lesson 1 – Exit Ticket”. Short written responses saved in Class Notebook.
Final minutes Plenary Summarise: “CS builds it. IT uses it. Both make technology work.” Invite a few pupil reflections to close lesson positively. Students share which path interests them most and why.

Resources and Preparation

  • lesson1.html (projected or on student devices)
  • lesson1.png Do Now image
  • Access to MazeGenerator.net
  • PowerPoint / Google Slides for IT task
  • Class Notebook structure set up for CS and IT sections

Support and Extension

  • Support: Provide a move bank (N/E/S/W) or slide template with placeholders.
  • Extension: Challenge students to optimise their maze sequence or adapt their slide for a different audience (e.g. parents).
  • SEND: Model examples if helpful, allow pairs, ensure large fonts and clear contrast.

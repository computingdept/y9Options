Teacher Guidance – Lesson 1
The Digital World: Where Do Computer Science and IT Meet?

Overview

Duration: 60 minutes    Class: Year 9 Options Taster

Focus: Understand the difference/overlap between Computer Science (creating/programming) and IT (using/managing tools), then try one CS and one IT task.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain what Computer Science and Information Technology mean.
  • Identify real examples of each in everyday life.
  • Describe where the two subjects overlap.

Lesson Flow & Timings (60 min)

Do Now routine: Students complete in Class Notebook / OneNote under “Lesson 1 – Do Now”, in silence. No discussion until you say “Reveal answers.”
Time Stage Teacher Guidance (modelling + prompts) Student Outcome
0–5 Do Now (silent in OneNote) Display the Do Now tab (image + 4 statements). Remind: “Work silently in OneNote.”
Circulate. After 3–4 mins, click Reveal and check quickly: ask “Why CS?” or “Why IT?” for one item. 		Answers typed in OneNote under “Lesson 1 – Do Now”.
5–10 Lesson focus Pose the anchor: “Where do CS and IT meet?”
Simple frame: CS builds it; IT uses it well. Both need problem-solving. 		Students can state the high-level difference.
10–18 Knowledge Recap Use your two Knowledge boxes from the lesson page:
• CS – Algorithms (robot chef / recipe analogy).
• IT – Workflows (school play analogy).
Quick questions: “What is an algorithm?”, “What breaks a workflow?” 		Shared understanding of algorithm vs workflow.
18–23 Model CS activity Project MazeGenerator.net.
Generate a small maze. On the board, write a demo path (e.g. N E E S E N).
Narrate testing step-by-step; when it fails, say “debug → fix one step”. Show a shorter, cleaner sequence. 		Students see what “good” looks like; understand debugging.
23–33 CS activity – Maze Navigator Let them work. Circulate with two prompts: “Read your steps aloud.” “Where did it go wrong? Fix just that.” Algorithm written, tested, and improved.
33–36 Model IT activity Open a blank Slide/PowerPoint. Type title Stay Safe Online + add one lock icon + 3 bullets.
Model accessibility: 32–40pt headings, high contrast, few words. 		Students know layout and success criteria.
36–46 IT activity – Online safety slide Direct to IT Activity tab. If needed, click “Show Sample Answers”. Remind positive tone and readability. One slide with title, 3–4 tips, simple visual.
46–52 Key Vocabulary (later for pace) Now formalise terms after usage. Read/quiz the vocab tab (Algorithm, Program, Hardware, Software, Data).
Tip: “Point to where you used each term today.” 		Terms anchored to concrete experience.
52–58 Exit Ticket Display 3Qs. Ask for full-sentence answers. Optional: collect via Forms or OneNote page. Identify (CS + IT), Explain difference, Reflect preference.
58–60 Plenary Reinforce: “CS builds it. IT uses it. Both make tech work.” Invite 2–3 share-outs. Students articulate which path interests them and why.

Support, Stretch & SEND

  • Support: Provide a tiny move bank (N/E/S/W). Offer a scaffolded slide with title box + 3 bullet placeholders.
  • Stretch: Ask for a shorter maze algorithm (optimise) or a slide variant for a different audience (parents/KS2).
  • SEND: Read instructions aloud; model one of each task; allow paired work; keep fonts large (≥24pt) and high contrast.
Implementation note: In the student page, consider moving the Key Vocabulary section beneath the IT activity to keep lesson energy high. (No JS changes required.)

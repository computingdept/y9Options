Knowledge Recap

üß† Computer Science ‚Äì Algorithms

An algorithm is just a clear set of steps that tell a computer exactly what to do. If one step is wrong or missing, the whole thing can fail.

  • Algorithms are like recipes ‚Äî follow each step in order to get the right result.
  • They use inputs (what goes in), processes (the actions), and outputs (the result).
  • Good algorithms are precise, efficient, and easy to debug.

ü™Ñ Analogy: Think of a robot chef following a cake recipe ‚Äî if the recipe says ‚Äúbake before adding eggs,‚Äù you‚Äôll get a mess! Computers need clear, ordered instructions too.

üßæ IT ‚Äì Workflows and Processes

In IT we often plan a workflow ‚Äî how people and technology work together to finish a task.

  • A workflow shows who does each part, when it happens, and what tools are used.
  • It helps keep jobs organised and stops things being missed.
  • Digital tools (like Forms, Teams, or Google Drive) help people follow a shared workflow.

ü™Ñ Analogy: A workflow is like running a school play ‚Äî everyone has their part, the cues must be in order, and when one person forgets a line, the show stops!

Lesson 1 – The Digital World: Where Do CS and IT Meet?

Do Now

Match each statement to Computer Science or IT.

1.
Designs and builds programs.
Answer: Computer Science
2.
Uses software to analyse business data.
Answer: IT
3.
Understands algorithms and logic.
Answer: Computer Science
4.
Creates digital graphics or presentations.
Answer: IT

What you'll learn

  • What Computer Science (CS) focuses on (algorithms, programming, data, hardware).
  • What Information Technology (IT) focuses on (using systems, productivity tools, digital content).
  • Where CS and IT overlap in school, work, and daily life.

How you'll know you’ve got it

  • You can correctly classify tasks as CS or IT and justify your choice.
  • You can explain an algorithm example (e.g., making a jam sandwich) and relate it to CS.
  • You can name two IT tasks you did today and two CS concepts from the lesson.

Key Vocabulary

Algorithm
A precise step-by-step method to solve a problem.
Program
A set of instructions a computer can execute.
Data
Symbols, numbers, text, images or sounds that a computer processes.
Hardware
The physical parts of a computer system.
Software
Programs and apps that run on hardware.
Productivity tools
IT applications like spreadsheets, word processors and slides.

Activities

  1. Classify Cards (CS vs IT): In pairs, sort 12 task cards into CS, IT or Overlap. Justify one tricky card.
  2. Mini-algorithm: Write 6 steps for an everyday task. Swap and “debug” with a partner.
  3. Exit sentence: “CS is mainly about …; IT is mainly about …; they overlap when …”.

Plenary

Cold-call: pick one task from today and explain whether it’s CS, IT, or overlap (with one reason).

What you'll learn today

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Describe what AI, Robotics, and Quantum mean at a simple level.
  • Give examples of how new tech can help people.
  • Explain how to use tech responsibly and lower your carbon footprint.

How you'll know you've got it

  • Spot where CS (building tech) and IT (using tech) both matter.
  • Share one benefit and one risk of an emerging tech.
  • Suggest two practical steps for greener computing.

Key Vocabulary

Artificial Intelligence (AI) — systems that learn patterns and make predictions.
Machine Learning — a way computers learn from data instead of rules alone.
Robotics — machines that sense, think, and act in the real world.
Quantum computing — uses qubits that can be 0 and 1 at once.
Bias — unfair patterns in data or decisions.
Carbon footprint — total greenhouse gases caused by an activity.
E‑waste — discarded electrical or electronic equipment.
Lifecycle — design → use → reuse → recycle.
Accessibility — designing so everyone can use it.

Main Activities

CS Track – Explore Emerging Tech

  1. AI: Write 3 features that could help in a homework helper app.
  2. Robotics: Plan sensors + actions for a litter‑picking robot.
  3. Quantum: In one sentence, how are qubits different from bits?
# Example: A qubit can be in a mix of 0 and 1 states,
# which may speed up some kinds of problems.

IT Track – Greener Tech Use

  1. Make a mini plan to cut energy: power settings, dark mode, tidy tabs.
  2. Choose cloud storage settings: avoid duplicates, use version history.
  3. Design a quick poster: "Use Tech, Waste Less" with 3 practical tips.

Compare & Reflect

  • Where do AI/robots help reduce waste or time?
  • What risks come with new tech (bias, misuse, energy use)?
  • How can smart IT practices reduce the impact?

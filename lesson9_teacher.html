Teacher Guidance – Lesson 9: Cybersecurity (CS) vs Digital Footprint (IT)

KS3 (Year 9 Options strand) • Pairs/individual work • OneNote evidence throughout

Overview

Intent (lesson purpose)

  • Understand common cyber threats (focus: phishing) and practical defences.
  • Explain a digital footprint and make safer choices when sharing online.
  • Apply rules (passwords, privacy, reporting) to real-looking scenarios.

Success criteria

  • Correctly classify examples as safe vs phish with a justified action.
  • Identify three places a footprint appears and improve at least one privacy setting.
  • Record evidence in Microsoft OneNote (Class Notebook) using the Snipping Tool.
Students work in Microsoft OneNote (no exercise books). Evidence for both CS and IT activities must be captured with Win + Shift + S (Snipping Tool) and pasted into the correct lesson section.

Timing & Flow (approx.)

  1. Silent Do Now 5–7 min — retrieval across L6–L8 + today’s hook.
  2. Mini-teach 6–8 min — phishing tells, digital footprint overview. Keep brisk.
  3. CS Activity: Spot the Phish 12–15 min — classify, justify, OneNote evidence.
  4. IT Activity: Footprint Audit 12–15 min — checklist + privacy change + evidence.
  5. Exit Ticket 3–5 min — quick check, collect answers verbally or via Forms.

Modelling is optional and at teacher judgement — use only if the class needs a worked example before independent practice.

Do Now (teacher script)

In silence: “Complete the Do Now in OneNote → Class Notebook → Lesson 9 – Do Now. No talking until I say.”

Reveal answers with the page toggle when ready. Cold-call 2–3 students to justify an answer in one sentence.

Check for understanding: If several students confuse database vs spreadsheet, note for recap next lesson.

Mini-teach (concise)

  • Phishing tells: urgency, threats, “verify now”, odd links, asking for passwords/money, poor spelling, unfamiliar sender.
  • Defences: long unique passwords (or passphrases), 2FA, hover to inspect links, update software, report/block.
  • Digital footprint: posts, pics, comments, likes, tags, usernames, metadata. Future you (colleges/employers) may see it.
Optional quick model (2–3 min): Show one fake email. Ask: “Safe or phish? What action?” Take one student justification.

CS Activity – Spot the Phish

Students classify four messages as Safe/Phish and state the correct action. Evidence goes to OneNote → Lesson 9 – CS Activity.

Non-negotiable evidence: Snip each example + typed decision and action in OneNote.

Modelling (optional)

  • Work a single example live: highlight trigger words (e.g., “urgent”, “verify”, “gift”).
  • Model a one-line justification: “Phish – urgent tone + unknown link → report and delete”.

Anticipated misconceptions

  • Thinking all security-code texts are scams. Clarify: code is fine if you requested it. If not, change password + review logins.
  • Believing staff will ever ask for passwords. They won’t.

Quick AfL

  • Mini-whiteboard or thumbs: “A/B/C/D — which is most obviously phishing?”
  • Listen for action verbs: report, block, verify independently.

IT Activity – Digital Footprint Audit

Pairs complete the checklist, improve a privacy setting (real or mock), and capture before/after into OneNote → Lesson 9 – IT Activity.

Evidence: Snips of the setting page (with sensitive details redacted if needed) + a one-sentence rationale.

Stretch

  • Create a 3-rule “posting policy” for yourself (audience, consent, location).
  • Draft a one-minute advisory for Y7 about phishing and footprints.

Support

  • Provide a model checklist and sentence starters: “I changed… because… This reduces risk of…”.
  • Offer screenshots of a demo account if platform access is restricted.

Assessment & Exit

Formative

  • Circulate during CS/IT tasks; require decision + action language for phishing.
  • Spot-check OneNote: look for both image evidence and written justification.

Exit Ticket (marking guidance)

  • Q1 (2 marks): Two phishing signs (e.g., urgent tone / strange link / asks for credentials / unknown sender).
  • Q2 (1 mark): Digital footprint definition (online info linked to a person).
  • Q3 (1 mark): One practical change (turn on 2FA / set profile to friends-only / stop location sharing / stronger passphrase).

Optional quick poll

Use Microsoft Forms for the exit ticket; export summary to OneNote afterwards.

Adaptive Teaching & Inclusion

  • Access: Provide large-print screenshots; read-aloud of example messages if needed.
  • Chunking: One example at a time; sentence frames for the action: “This is phish because… I will …”.
  • Language: Keep vocabulary board visible: phishing, 2FA, privacy, footprint.
  • Devices: Allow pair-share roles (navigator/recorder) to reduce cognitive load.

Setup & Resources

  • Student page: lesson9.html (Cybersecurity vs Digital Footprint).
  • All students logged into Microsoft OneNote (Class Notebook).
  • Snipping Tool hotkey on board: Win + Shift + S.
  • Optional: Microsoft Forms link for Exit Ticket.
Plan B (no internet): Print the four phishing messages and the footprint checklist. Students annotate and then photograph/upload to OneNote later.

Safeguarding & e-Safety Reminders

  • Reinforce: never share passwords; staff will not ask for them.
  • Reporting pathways: platform Report/Block, tutor/HOY, school safeguarding lead.
  • Remind students not to share personal account screenshots publicly; crop/redact names where appropriate.

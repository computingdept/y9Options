Knowledge Recap

üß† Computer Science ‚Äì Flowcharts & Decisions

A flowchart shows the steps of an algorithm using symbols and arrows. It helps programmers plan and check logic before writing any code.

  • Each shape means something: oval = start/end, parallelogram = input/output, diamond = decision.
  • Arrows show the flow of control ‚Äî the order that steps happen in.
  • Decisions use Yes / No branches (like ‚ÄúIf it‚Äôs raining ‚Üí take an umbrella‚Äù).

ü™Ñ Analogy: A flowchart is like a choose-your-own-adventure story ‚Äî every time you reach a choice, you follow a new path until you reach the ending.

üìã IT ‚Äì Digital Forms & Data Flow

In IT, forms collect and pass information through a workflow automatically. Each question or box becomes a piece of data that moves to the next step.

  • Digital forms make data entry faster and reduce mistakes.
  • They can trigger actions ‚Äî like sending emails or saving results to a spreadsheet.
  • Well-designed forms are short, clear, and include validation (checking the answers make sense).

ü™Ñ Analogy: A form is like a mail sorter ‚Äî each answer goes into the right slot so the information ends up where it belongs.

Key Vocabulary

Algorithm
A precise, step-by-step method a computer can follow to solve a problem.
Workflow
The ordered sequence of tasks people and tools follow to complete work.
Decision
A yes/no (boolean) branch that changes which step happens next.
Input / Output
Information that goes into / comes out of a system or process.
